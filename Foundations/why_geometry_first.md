# why_geometry_first.md
### *Why ED is grounded in geometry rather than equations*

ED is a geometry‑first ontology.  
This is a philosophical stance, not a mathematical one.

---

## 1. Geometry is structural
Geometry describes:

- relations  
- contrasts  
- separations  
- alignments  
- patterns  

These are the **first facts** of ED.

---

## 2. Equations are descriptive, not foundational
Equations describe:

- how structure evolves  
- how influence propagates  
- how motifs reinforce  

But equations cannot define:

- what exists  
- what counts as structure  
- what the ontology is made of  

Geometry can.

---

## 3. Geometry avoids background assumptions
Equations often assume:

- coordinates  
- metrics  
- continuity  
- differentiability  

ED assumes none of these.

Geometry in ED is:

- relational  
- contrast‑based  
- motif‑driven  
- commitment‑constrained  

---

## 4. Geometry is pre‑mathematical
In ED, geometry is not:

- metric  
- coordinate‑based  
- embedded  
- algebraic  

It is **structural**.

Geometry is the pattern of contrast created by primitive interactions.

---

## 5. Summary
ED is geometry‑first because:

- structure precedes description  
- relations precede equations  
- contrast precedes dynamics  
- ontology precedes mathematics  

Geometry is the **first layer** of ED.
