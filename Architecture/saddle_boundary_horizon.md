# **Saddle → Boundary → Horizon**
### *The core architectural triad of ED*

This document defines the three structural layers that emerge when ED primitives interact.  
They are not physical objects, not fields, not equations — they are **architectural roles** that appear whenever gradients, multiplicity, and commitments combine.

This triad is the *structural heart* of ED.

---

# **1. Saddles — the geometry**

A **saddle** is the fundamental geometric form produced when ED gradients flip sign across orthogonal directions.

A saddle is where:

- one gradient direction is increasing  
- another is decreasing  
- the system cannot “choose” a single monotonic direction  
- structure becomes unavoidable  

Conceptually:

- Saddles are **where geometry lives**.  
- They are the **sites of structural tension**.  
- They are the **points where motifs begin**.  

A saddle is not a surface or a shape — it is a **pattern of gradient relations**.

**Key architectural role:**  
Saddles are the *geometric seeds* from which all higher structure emerges.

---

# **2. Boundaries — the topology**

A **boundary** forms when a saddle’s gradient sign‑flip stabilizes across a region.

Boundaries are:

- the **topological consequences** of saddle geometry  
- the **places where commitments change**  
- the **interfaces between motifs**  
- the **structural “edges” of ED space”**  

A boundary is not a wall or a surface — it is a **topological distinction** created by persistent gradient contrast.

Conceptually:

- Boundaries partition structure.  
- Boundaries define “inside vs outside” without coordinates.  
- Boundaries are the **organizational scaffolding** of ED.  

**Key architectural role:**  
Boundaries turn local geometric tension into **global topological structure**.

---

# **3. Horizons — the dynamics**

A **horizon** forms when a boundary becomes directional — when the structure implies a “before/after” or “toward/away” relation.

Horizons are:

- the **dynamic consequences** of boundaries  
- the **places where commitments propagate**  
- the **structural origin of timing relations**  
- the **edges of influence**  

A horizon is not a physical horizon — it is a **directional constraint** created by the architecture.

Conceptually:

- Horizons encode **what can affect what**.  
- Horizons define **structural causality**.  
- Horizons are the **dynamic layer** of ED.  

**Key architectural role:**  
Horizons turn topological distinctions into **directed structure** — the beginnings of dynamics.

---

# **4. The triad as a pipeline**

The three layers form a forced architectural pipeline:

```
Saddle  →  Boundary  →  Horizon
geometry    topology     dynamics
```

This pipeline is not optional.  
It is the **universal consequence** of:

- gradient sign‑flips  
- multiplicity  
- commitments  
- structural propagation  

Whenever the primitives interact, this triad appears.

---

# **5. Why this triad matters**

This triad is the **minimal architecture** that allows:

- structure  
- partition  
- direction  
- influence  
- propagation  
- motif formation  

It is the **bridge** between:

- raw primitives  
- emergent structure  

It is the **architectural backbone** of ED.
