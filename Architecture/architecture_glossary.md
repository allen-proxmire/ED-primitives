# **Architecture Glossary**
### *Core structural terms used throughout the ED‑Primitives architecture*

This glossary defines the architectural vocabulary used across the ED‑Primitives repository.  
These terms describe **structural roles**, **relations**, and **patterns** — not physics, not equations, not PDEs, and not empirical content.

This file keeps the architecture folder self‑contained and readable.

---

# **Saddle**

A structural configuration created when gradients flip sign across orthogonal directions.  
A saddle is the **geometric seed** of ED structure — the first stable form produced by primitive interactions.

Key idea: **geometry born from contrast**.

---

# **Boundary**

A persistent structural interface created when a saddle’s gradient contrast stabilizes across a region.  
Boundaries are **topological distinctions** — the organizational scaffolding of ED.

Key idea: **topology born from stabilized contrast**.

---

# **Horizon**

A directional structural interface created when a boundary acquires asymmetry.  
Horizons encode **what can influence what**, forming the dynamic layer of ED.

Key idea: **dynamics born from directed contrast**.

---

# **Gradient Sign‑Flip**

The fundamental architectural generator of structure.  
A sign‑flip occurs when directional tendencies invert relative to each other, forcing the formation of saddles, boundaries, and horizons.

Key idea: **contrast is the engine of structure**.

---

# **Motif**

A stable, recurring structural pattern formed from repeated saddle‑boundary‑horizon triads.  
Motifs are the **first emergent building blocks** of ED architecture.

Key idea: **patterns that persist**.

---

# **Structural Propagation**

The architectural process by which motifs, boundaries, and horizons extend, reinforce, or interlock to form larger‑scale structure.

Key idea: **local patterns scaling upward**.

---

# **Commitments**

Structural obligations encoded by the primitives that constrain how motifs and boundaries propagate.  
Commitments are not choices — they are **architectural necessities**.

Key idea: **structure must follow its commitments**.

---

# **Multiplicity**

The architectural condition that multiple structural tendencies coexist and interact.  
Multiplicity prevents collapse into uniformity and enables rich motif formation.

Key idea: **structure requires many interacting tendencies**.

---

# **Contrast**

A difference in directional tendencies that forces structure.  
Contrast is the precursor to sign‑flips, saddles, boundaries, and horizons.

Key idea: **difference drives architecture**.

---

# **Triad (Saddle–Boundary–Horizon)**

The universal architectural pipeline produced by primitive interactions:

```
Saddle → Boundary → Horizon
geometry   topology   dynamics
```

This triad is the **core structural sequence** of ED.

---

# **Primitive Interaction**

Any architectural relation between primitives that produces gradients, contrast, commitments, or structural propagation.  
These interactions are the **raw substrate** from which all structure emerges.

Key idea: **primitives interacting = architecture forming**.

---

# **Structural Network**

A large‑scale pattern formed when motifs and boundaries interconnect.  
Networks are the **global architecture** built from local triads.

Key idea: **global structure from local rules**.

---

# **Architecture**

The total structural organization produced by the primitives — including motifs, boundaries, horizons, and networks.  
Architecture is not physics; it is the **ontology** that physics will later inhabit.

Key idea: **the ED world’s structural skeleton**.
