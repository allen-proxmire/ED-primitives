
**How the ED Primitives Combine Into Saddles, Boundaries, Horizons, and Motifs**

This document defines the **interaction rules** of the Event Density (ED) ontology — the conceptual “grammar” that governs how the 13 primitives combine into higher‑level structures.

It is **purely architectural**.  
No equations.  
No PDE.  
No physics.  
No experiments.  
No platform bridges.

These are the **constitutional rules** for how ED builds structure from its primitives.

---

# 1. Interaction Rule Philosophy

The primitives are **not** independent.  
They are designed to **compose**.

Each primitive:

- constrains  
- enables  
- shapes  
- or modulates  

the behavior of others.

The interaction rules describe **how**.

Think of this document as the **syntax** of the ED ontology.

---

# 2. Local Interaction Rules (ED‑01 → ED‑05)

These rules govern how micro‑events form local structure.

### **Rule L1 — Micro‑events must join chains**  
`ED‑01 + ED‑02`  
A micro‑event is only meaningful when linked into a chain.  
There are no isolated events in ED.

### **Rule L2 — Participation determines chain membership**  
`ED‑03`  
An event participates in exactly the chains it expresses.  
Participation is the “binding rule.”

### **Rule L3 — Bandwidth limits participation**  
`ED‑04`  
A chain cannot accept unlimited participation.  
Bandwidth is the first structural constraint.

### **Rule L4 — Local density emerges from participation**  
`ED‑03 + ED‑04 → ED‑05`  
Event density is not a substance — it is the *result* of participation patterns.

These rules define the **substrate**.

---

# 3. Gradient Interaction Rules (ED‑06 → ED‑09)

These rules govern how structure begins to form.

### **Rule G1 — Gradients arise from density differences**  
`ED‑05 → ED‑06`  
A gradient is a directional change in event density.

### **Rule G2 — Channels form along stable gradients**  
`ED‑06 + ED‑07`  
A channel is a coherent flow of events aligned with a gradient.

### **Rule G3 — Multiplicity allows overlapping channels**  
`ED‑07 + ED‑08`  
Multiple channels can coexist in the same region without merging.

### **Rule G4 — Tension polarity determines compression vs expansion**  
`ED‑06 + ED‑09`  
Gradients have polarity:  
- compression (inward)  
- expansion (outward)

These rules define the **geometry** of the ED manifold.

---

# 4. Global Interaction Rules (ED‑10 → ED‑13)

These rules govern how structure stabilizes and persists.

### **Rule O1 — Individuation creates boundaries**  
`ED‑10`  
A region becomes “itself” when individuation stabilizes its identity.

### **Rule O2 — Commitment preserves structure over time**  
`ED‑10 + ED‑11`  
A boundary persists only if commitment reinforces it.

### **Rule O3 — Thickening creates regimes**  
`ED‑11 + ED‑12`  
When commitment accumulates, a region enters a “thickened” regime with stable behavior.

### **Rule O4 — Relational timing coordinates chains**  
`ED‑02 + ED‑13`  
Timing is not absolute — it is relational across chains.

These rules define the **global organization** of ED.

---

# 5. Emergent Motif Rules

From the interactions above, ED generates universal motifs.

### **Motif M1 — Saddles**  
`ED‑06 + ED‑09 + ED‑10`  
A saddle occurs where gradients flip sign across orthogonal directions and individuation stabilizes the crossing.

### **Motif M2 — Boundaries**  
`ED‑10 + ED‑11`  
A boundary is a region where individuation and commitment reinforce each other.

### **Motif M3 — Horizons**  
`ED‑06 + ED‑12`  
A horizon is where gradients propagate outward into a thickened regime, making internal structure unresolvable.

### **Motif M4 — Channels**  
`ED‑07 + ED‑04 + ED‑11`  
A channel is a coherent, bandwidth‑limited, commitment‑stabilized flow of events.

### **Motif M5 — Filaments, Sheets, Voids**  
`ED‑08 + ED‑09 + ED‑12`  
Multiplicity + polarity + thickening generate the three universal structural motifs.

These motifs are **structural**, not physical.  
They appear in physics only after the PDE layer is added (outside this repo).

# 6. Composition Rules (How Motifs Combine)

### **Rule C1 — Saddles connect to channels**  
A saddle is a junction where channels diverge or converge.

### **Rule C2 — Boundaries enclose thickened regions**  
A thickened regime is always bounded by an individuated surface.

### **Rule C3 — Horizons cap expansion**  
A horizon marks the limit of resolvable structure.

### **Rule C4 — Multiplicity enables networks**  
Overlapping channels form networks of motifs.

### **Rule C5 — Timing synchronizes motifs**  
Relational timing coordinates motif interactions across chains.

These rules define the **architecture of complex structure**.

# 7. Forbidden Interactions (to prevent category errors)

To keep the ontology clean:

- Micro‑events do **not** have location or momentum.  
- Chains do **not** imply trajectories.  
- Gradients do **not** imply forces.  
- Channels do **not** imply particles.  
- Boundaries do **not** imply surfaces in space.  
- Horizons do **not** imply black holes.  
- Timing does **not** imply clocks.

These are **conceptual**, not physical, structures.
