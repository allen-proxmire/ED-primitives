# interpretive_principles.md
### *The core interpretive commitments of ED*

These principles define the conceptual posture required to read ED correctly.  
They are not laws, equations, or physical assumptions — they are **interpretive commitments**.

---

## 1. Ontology before dynamics
ED defines **what exists** before describing **how it behaves**.

Interpretive rule:
> **Structure first, evolution second.**

---

## 2. Geometry before mathematics
ED begins with **geometric relations**, not with equations.

Interpretive rule:
> **Patterns precede formulas.**

---

## 3. Events before objects
Objects are stabilized event patterns, not primitives.

Interpretive rule:
> **Events are the atoms of ontology.**

---

## 4. Relations before absolutes
Nothing in ED exists independently.

Interpretive rule:
> **Participation defines existence.**

---

## 5. Motifs before mechanisms
Behavior arises from motif expression, not from forces.

Interpretive rule:
> **Patterns explain behavior.**

---

## 6. Regimes before states
ED does not use instantaneous states.

Interpretive rule:
> **Context determines behavior.**

---

## 7. Coordination before time
Timing is relational, not absolute.

Interpretive rule:
> **Time is coordination, not a background.**

---

## 8. Composition before reduction
Understanding ED means understanding how primitives compose.

Interpretive rule:
> **Build upward, don’t break downward.**

---

## 9. Structure before substance
ED does not assume material entities.

Interpretive rule:
> **Substance is emergent.**

---

## 10. Architecture before physics
ED is the substrate; physics is layered on top.

Interpretive rule:
> **Ontology is prior to theory.**

---
