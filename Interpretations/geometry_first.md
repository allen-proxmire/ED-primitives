# geometry_first.md
### *Why ED begins with structure, not equations*

ED is a geometry‑first ontology.  
This means the ontology begins with **structural relations**, not with mathematical laws.

Most scientific frameworks start with:

- equations  
- operators  
- metrics  
- symmetries  
- dynamical rules  

ED reverses this order.

---

## 1. Geometry precedes mathematics

In ED, the primitives define:

- gradients  
- channels  
- polarity  
- multiplicity  
- individuation  
- boundaries  
- horizons  

These are **geometric structures**, not algebraic objects.

Interpretation rule:

> **Geometry is what exists.  
> Mathematics is how we later describe its evolution.**

The PDE (in the main repo) is a *description* of how structure changes —  
not the foundation of the ontology.

---

## 2. Geometry precedes dynamics

Dynamics require:

- direction  
- propagation  
- influence  
- timing  

But these only make sense **after** structure exists.

ED’s ordering:

1. **Saddles** (geometry)  
2. **Boundaries** (topology)  
3. **Horizons** (dynamics)  

This is not a physical claim — it is an **ontological ordering**.

---

## 3. Geometry precedes interpretation

ED does not interpret geometry as:

- curvature  
- metric structure  
- spacetime  
- embedding  

Those belong to physics.

In ED, geometry is simply:

> **the pattern of contrast created by primitive interactions.**

---

## 4. Geometry precedes coordinates

ED does not assume:

- coordinate systems  
- distances  
- angles  
- metrics  

Geometry is **relational**, not metric.

Coordinates are a later convenience, not a primitive.

---

## 5. Summary

To think geometry‑first:

- Think **patterns**, not equations.  
- Think **contrast**, not curvature.  
- Think **structure**, not metric.  
- Think **relations**, not coordinates.  
- Think **architecture**, not dynamics.  

Geometry is the **first layer** of the ED ontology.
