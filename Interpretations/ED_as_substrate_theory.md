# ED_as_substrate_theory.md
### *What it means for ED to be a substrate theory*

ED is not a physical theory.  
It is a **substrate theory** — a description of the structural world that physics will later inhabit.

This document explains what that means.

---

## 1. A substrate theory defines what exists, not how it behaves
ED defines:

- primitives  
- gradients  
- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  
- regimes  
- commitments  

These are **ontological structures**, not physical laws.

Physics comes later.

---

## 2. ED provides the architecture, not the equations
The PDE (in the main repo) describes:

- propagation  
- evolution  
- influence  
- coordination  

But the PDE is **not** part of the ontology.  
It is a *description* of how the ontology behaves.

Interpretation rule:

> **ED defines the world.  
> Physics describes its behavior.**

---

## 3. ED is prior to spacetime, fields, and particles
ED does not assume:

- spacetime  
- fields  
- particles  
- operators  
- Hilbert spaces  

These are **derived structures** that emerge when physics is layered on top of ED.

ED is the **substrate** that makes such structures possible.

---

## 4. ED is not a replacement for physics
ED does not attempt to:

- unify physics  
- replace physics  
- reinterpret physics  
- derive all physical laws  

ED provides the **structural foundation** on which physics can be built.

Physics remains physics.

---

## 5. ED is not metaphysics
ED is not a metaphysical system.  
It does not make claims about:

- meaning  
- experience  
- consciousness  
- purpose  

ED is **structural ontology**, not metaphysical speculation.

---

## 6. Summary

To understand ED as a substrate theory:

- ED defines **structure**, not behavior  
- ED defines **ontology**, not dynamics  
- ED defines **architecture**, not equations  
- ED defines **relations**, not observables  
- ED defines **possibility**, not prediction  

Physics lives **on top of** ED — not inside it.
