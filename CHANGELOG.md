# Changelog — ED Primitives

All notable constitutional changes to this repository are recorded here.
Physics-sector progress is tracked in the companion `event-density`
repository and is **not** logged here.

The format follows semantic versioning at the constitutional level:

- **Major (vN.0)** — change to the primitive set, the form-FORCED /
  value-INHERITED commitment, or the structural ceiling.
- **Minor (vN.M)** — new foundational memos, clarifications, glossary
  additions that do not alter the primitives or the commitments.
- **Patch (vN.M.P)** — typographical or expository corrections only.

## Amendment Rules

Amendments to this constitution are governed by the following rules.

1. **Primitives are amendment-only.** No primitive may be added, removed,
   renamed, or have its scope changed except through a numbered amendment
   that bumps the major version.

2. **Commitments are amendment-only.** The form-FORCED / value-INHERITED
   commitment and the structural ceiling are amendment-only at the major
   level.

3. **Amendments are explicit.** Every amendment must:
   - Bump the appropriate version component.
   - Include a dated entry in this file.
   - State the rationale in plain language.
   - Cite the foundational memo or theorem-level work that motivated it
     (typically a result from the `event-density` repository).

4. **No silent revisions.** Editing a primitive memo to change its scope,
   even subtly, is a constitutional change and must be logged here. Pure
   wording or typographical fixes are patch-level.

5. **Forward-compatible by default.** Where an amendment can be expressed
   as an addition (a new foundational memo, a new derived concept) rather
   than a modification, the additive form is preferred.

6. **Empirical findings do not amend.** Numerical results from physics
   sector work never trigger a constitutional amendment by themselves,
   because ED's commitment is that values are inherited. Only a *structural*
   discovery — one that changes what is FORCED — can motivate an amendment.
