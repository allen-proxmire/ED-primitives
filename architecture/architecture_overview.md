**How the ED Primitives Compose Into Structure**

This document explains the **internal architecture** of the Event Density (ED) ontology — how the 13 primitives relate, combine, and generate higher‑level structures.  
It is conceptual, not physical.  
No equations, no PDEs, no experiments, no platform bridges.

If the `primitives/` folder defines the **atoms** of ED, this document describes the **molecular rules**: how those atoms fit together to form channels, motifs, saddles, boundaries, and horizons.

This is the structural backbone of the ED ontology.

---

# 1. The Architecture in One Sentence  
**ED is an ontology where micro‑events participate in chains, shaped by gradients, constrained by bandwidth, organized into channels, modulated by multiplicity, polarized by tension, individuated by boundaries, stabilized by commitment, thickened into regimes, and coordinated by relational timing.**

Everything else emerges from these interactions.

---

# 2. The Three Architectural Layers

ED’s ontology organizes naturally into three layers:

## **Layer A — Local Units (ED‑01 → ED‑05)**  
These primitives define *what exists* at the smallest scale.

- **ED‑01 Micro‑Event** — the atomic unit of occurrence  
- **ED‑02 Chain** — events linked into sequences  
- **ED‑03 Participation** — how events join chains  
- **ED‑04 Participation Bandwidth** — limits on participation  
- **ED‑05 Event Density** — local concentration of events  

These five primitives define the **substrate**.

---

## **Layer B — Structural Forces (ED‑06 → ED‑09)**  
These primitives define *how structure forms*.

- **ED‑06 ED Gradient** — directional change in density  
- **ED‑07 Channel** — coherent flow of events  
- **ED‑08 Multiplicity** — overlapping channels  
- **ED‑09 Tension Polarity** — compression vs expansion  

These four primitives define the **geometry** of the manifold.

---

## **Layer C — Global Organization (ED‑10 → ED‑13)**  
These primitives define *how the manifold stabilizes and evolves*.

- **ED‑10 Individuation** — boundaries and identity  
- **ED‑11 Commitment** — persistence of structure  
- **ED‑12 Thickening** — regime formation  
- **ED‑13 Relational Timing** — coordination across chains  

These four primitives define the **global behavior** of ED.
