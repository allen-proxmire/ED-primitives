# The Dimensional Atlas

**Status:** constitutional foundation memo.
**Scope:** ontology-level only. No physics equations, no numerical content.

---

## 1. What the Dimensional Atlas Is

The **Dimensional Atlas** is the inheritance layer of Event Density (ED).
It is the place where numerical constants enter the framework — and
crucially, it is the only such place. ED itself does not generate
numerical content. It generates *structural slots*. The Dimensional
Atlas is the catalogue of those slots and a record of which empirical
constants the surrounding physics community has placed in each.

The Atlas is not a derivation. It is a bookkeeping discipline. Its
purpose is to make explicit that every numerical quantity appearing in
any downstream ED-built theory has been *imported* from empirical
physics, never *predicted* from the primitives.

---

## 2. What ED Does Not Predict

ED, considered at the constitutional level, does not predict any of
the following:

- The speed of light.
- The reduced Planck constant.
- Particle masses of any species.
- Electric charge or any other coupling.
- Newton's gravitational constant.
- The cosmological constant.
- The fine-structure constant or any dimensionless ratio.
- Mixing angles, decay widths, lifetimes.
- Any other empirically measured numerical quantity.

This list is not exhaustive. The constitutional rule is simpler than
any list: **no numerical constant is forced by the primitives.** Where
a constant appears in downstream work, it is imported from the Atlas
and labelled INHERITED.

---

## 3. Structural Slots vs Numerical Values

ED's primitives, taken together, define a structural skeleton. That
skeleton has *slots* — places where a number must enter for the
skeleton to become a concrete physical model. Examples of slots
(stated abstractly, with no physics content) include:

- a scale at which the discreteness of micro-events becomes resolvable;
- a bandwidth bounding participation across a chain;
- a characteristic interval of relational timing;
- amplitudes of cross-chain coupling.

The primitives *force the existence* of each such slot. The
Dimensional Atlas *records* which empirical number, if any, fills it
in a given physical theory. The two activities are kept rigorously
separate.

---

## 4. Why This Is a Constitutional Commitment

The Atlas is not a convenience. It is a constitutional commitment for
three reasons.

**First**, it preserves the honesty of the framework. ED claims a great
deal at the structural level. By stating in advance that it makes no
numerical claims, the framework eliminates a class of false
attributions: no measured constant can be cited as evidence either for
or against the primitives, because the primitives never spoke to that
number.

**Second**, it preserves modularity. Because ED supplies only the form
of the structural skeleton, multiple empirical fillings of the slots
are formally compatible with the same primitive set. The framework
does not break if a constant is re-measured.

**Third**, it preserves the boundary between ontology and physics.
Without the Atlas, the natural drift of a structural ontology is
toward over-claiming — toward asserting that the framework predicts
the very numbers that, in fact, it has imported. The Atlas makes that
drift visible and refusable.

---

## 5. How the Atlas Operates in Practice

The Dimensional Atlas is operationalised by a single discipline:
every numerical quantity that appears in any ED-derived work must be
labelled with one of two tags.

- **FORCED** — applies to *forms* only: equations, classifications,
  kernel classes, theorems. Never to numbers.
- **INHERITED** — applies to *values* only: any number, magnitude,
  dimensionless ratio, mass, coupling, amplitude, threshold.

A document that produces a number from the primitives alone has
violated the constitutional commitment. A document that imports a
number from empirical physics and labels it INHERITED is in good
standing.

The companion memo `form_forced_value_inherited.md` develops this
discipline in full.

---

## 6. Relationship to the `event-density` Repository

The Dimensional Atlas itself, as a working catalogue, is maintained in
the companion `event-density` repository, where it tracks the
empirical constants relevant to specific physics-sector constructions
(quantum, relativistic, gravitational, cosmological). This memo
establishes only the *constitutional principle*: that such an Atlas
exists, that it is the only port of entry for numerical content, and
that the primitives do not bypass it.
