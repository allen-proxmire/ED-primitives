# why_gradients.md
### *Why gradients are the calculus of ED*

Gradients in ED are not mathematical derivatives.  
They are **directional tendencies** — structural inclinations encoded by the primitives.

---

## 1. Gradients express contrast
Contrast is the generator of structure.

Gradients encode:

- up vs down  
- toward vs away  
- reinforce vs oppose  

These contrasts produce:

- sign‑flips  
- saddles  
- boundaries  
- horizons  

---

## 2. Gradients are pre‑mathematical
Gradients in ED do not require:

- coordinates  
- metrics  
- continuity  
- differentiability  

They are **qualitative**, not quantitative.

---

## 3. Gradients drive motif formation
Motifs arise when gradients:

- align  
- oppose  
- reinforce  
- stabilize  

Gradients are the **calculus** of ED — the rules of structural change.

---

## 4. Summary
Gradients matter because:

- they encode contrast  
- they generate structure  
- they support motifs  
- they drive propagation  

Gradients are the **engine** of ED architecture.
