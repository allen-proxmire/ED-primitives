# The Structural Ceiling

**Status:** constitutional foundation memo.
**Scope:** ED's explicit non-claims.

---

## 1. Why a Ceiling

A serious ontology is defined as much by what it refuses to claim as
by what it asserts. Event Density operates with a deliberate
**structural ceiling**: an explicit, enumerated list of statements
the framework does *not* make, and is constitutionally forbidden from
making, on the basis of the primitives alone.

The ceiling is not a hedge. It is an identity-defining commitment.
Without the ceiling, ED would silently inherit the burdens of every
neighbouring framework and would be measured against claims it never
authored. With the ceiling, ED's actual scope — structural,
ontological, form-only — is clear and defensible.

---

## 2. What ED Does Not Claim

The following items are **non-claims**. ED may engage with them at
the level of structural compatibility or downstream construction in
the `event-density` repository, but it does not produce any of them
from the primitives alone.

### 2.1 Fundamental constants

ED does not predict the speed of light, the reduced Planck constant,
particle masses, electric charge or any other coupling, Newton's
gravitational constant, or any dimensionless ratio. Every numerical
constant entering any ED-built construction is INHERITED via the
Dimensional Atlas.

### 2.2 The Standard Model gauge group

ED does not predict the gauge group of the Standard Model. The
specific product structure of the empirical gauge group is not a
consequence of the primitives. ED may speak to the *form* of how
gauge-like structures can arise from the ontology; it does not select
a particular group from the primitives.

### 2.3 Generation count

ED does not predict the number of fermion generations. The empirical
count is not a primitive-level theorem. Any statement to the contrary
violates the constitutional ceiling.

### 2.4 The Higgs mechanism

ED does not predict the Higgs mechanism, the existence of a Higgs
sector, the electroweak scale, or the mass-generation route used in
the Standard Model. Mass enters ED downstream as an INHERITED value
filling a structural slot.

### 2.5 The Einstein field equations

ED does not derive the Einstein field equations from the primitives.
The primitives may underwrite kinematic structures that are
*compatible* with a metric description, but the dynamical content of
general relativity — its specific equations of motion for geometry —
is not a primitive-level consequence.

### 2.6 The cosmological constant magnitude

ED does not predict the magnitude of the cosmological constant.
Form-level structures resembling a cosmological term may exist
downstream, but their numerical value is INHERITED, not FORCED.

### 2.7 The Newtonian limit

ED does not produce a Newtonian limit from primitives alone. Any
recovery of Newtonian behaviour requires inputs from the Dimensional
Atlas and lives in the physics sector, not the constitution.

### 2.8 Numerical predictions of any kind

ED does not, at the primitive level, produce any number. This
restatement of the form-FORCED / value-INHERITED rule is repeated
here because it is the most frequently violated boundary, and
because every more specific entry above is a consequence of it.

---

## 3. What the Ceiling Permits

The ceiling forbids certain claims; it does not forbid engagement.
ED is free to:

- show that its primitive set is *compatible* with the form of a
  given equation, structure, or sector;
- locate a structural slot that an empirical phenomenon occupies;
- characterise the admissible class of forms a downstream theory
  must lie within;
- supply an ontological reading of a working physical theory.

The ceiling draws the line at *production*. A statement that the
primitives produce a number, a specific gauge group, a specific
equation of motion, or any other empirical content crosses the line
and is rejected.

---

## 4. Why the Ceiling Is Essential to ED's Identity

Three reasons make the ceiling constitutive rather than optional.

**It defines what ED is for.** ED is an ontology — a specification of
what there is and how it is structured. An ontology that quietly
predicts numbers is no longer an ontology; it has slipped into being a
physical theory and inherits the obligations of one. The ceiling
preserves the ontological role.

**It defines what counts as evidence.** With the ceiling in place,
empirical findings about numbers neither confirm nor refute ED
directly. They confirm or refute the *physics-sector constructions*
built on top of ED. ED is tested at the structural level, by whether
its FORCED forms hold up. The ceiling makes the test specification
clear.

**It defines what counts as success.** Without the ceiling, success
would be measured by how many empirical numbers ED reproduces. With
the ceiling, success is measured by how many structural commitments
remain coherent and FORCED across new domains. These are different
yardsticks, and ED uses the second.

---

## 5. Maintenance

The ceiling is amendment-only. Adding to the list of non-claims is a
clarification and may be done at the minor-version level. Removing
from the list — that is, promoting some former non-claim to a FORCED
result — is a major-version event and requires the full amendment
procedure described in `CHANGELOG.md`. No item leaves this list
silently.
