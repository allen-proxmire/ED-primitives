# why_primitives.md
### *Why ED begins with primitives instead of laws*

ED starts with **primitives** because the ontology must define *what exists* before it can define *how anything behaves*.

This is a philosophical commitment, not a mathematical one.

---

## 1. Laws presuppose entities
A law describes:

- how something changes  
- how something interacts  
- how something evolves  

But laws cannot be written until we know:

- what the “something” is  
- what structure it has  
- what relations it participates in  

ED therefore begins with **primitives**, not laws.

---

## 2. Primitives define the ontology
Each primitive encodes:

- a structural role  
- a relational capacity  
- a compositional function  
- a constraint on architecture  

Together, the primitives define the **conceptual atoms** of ED.

---

## 3. Primitives prevent circularity
If ED began with equations, it would implicitly assume:

- coordinates  
- metrics  
- continuity  
- differentiability  
- background structure  

These assumptions would smuggle in a hidden ontology.

Primitives avoid this.

---

## 4. Primitives support compositionality
ED structure emerges from:

- motif formation  
- boundary reinforcement  
- horizon propagation  

These require **simple ingredients** that combine into **rich structure**.

Primitives are those ingredients.

---

## 5. Summary
ED begins with primitives because:

- ontology precedes dynamics  
- structure precedes law  
- composition precedes reduction  
- clarity precedes complexity  

Primitives are the **foundation** of the ED worldview.
