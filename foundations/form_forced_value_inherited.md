# Form-FORCED, Value-INHERITED

**Status:** constitutional foundation memo.
**Scope:** the central methodological discipline of Event Density.

---

## 1. The Discipline in One Line

> **Forms are FORCED. Values are INHERITED.**

This is the core methodological commitment of Event Density. It runs
through every layer of the framework — primitives, architecture,
foundations, interpretations, and all downstream physics-sector work.
Every claim ED makes can be located cleanly on one side of the line
or the other. Claims that try to straddle the line are constitutional
errors and are rejected.

---

## 2. What "FORCED" Means

A claim that something is **FORCED** means:

- it is a *form*, not a number;
- it follows from the primitives plus the constitutional commitments;
- no admissible alternative exists within the ontology.

Forms include: the structural shape of an equation, the
classification of an admissible class, the existence of a symmetry,
the necessity of a relational property, the typing of an interaction.
A form is FORCED when the primitives leave no room for it to be
otherwise.

What FORCED does *not* mean:

- It does not mean "the primitives produced a number."
- It does not mean "this matches experiment."
- It does not mean "this is the only theory consistent with the data."

FORCED is a structural verdict, internal to the ontology.

---

## 3. What "INHERITED" Means

A claim that something is **INHERITED** means:

- it is a *value*, not a form;
- it has been imported from empirical physics via the Dimensional Atlas;
- the primitives are agnostic about its specific magnitude.

Values include: numerical constants, dimensionless ratios, masses,
couplings, amplitudes, thresholds, characteristic scales, decay
rates, mixing angles, and every other number. A value is INHERITED
when it occupies a structural slot the primitives forced into
existence, but whose number the primitives do not determine.

What INHERITED does *not* mean:

- It does not mean "free parameter."
- It does not mean "guess."
- It does not mean "irrelevant."

An INHERITED number can be tightly constrained by experiment and can
be essential to a working physical model. The label simply records
that the number's source is empirical, not primitive.

---

## 4. Why the Rule Is Held Across All Layers

The discipline is invariant across layers because the layers are
ontologically continuous. There is no privileged level at which the
primitives "switch on" the production of numbers. Every level — from
the bare primitives, through the architectural composition rules,
through the foundational philosophical grounding, into the physics
sector — speaks only to *form*. Values enter exactly once, at the
inheritance port called the Dimensional Atlas.

Holding the rule across all layers has three effects:

1. **It prevents quiet drift.** Without a constitutional rule, the
   natural pressure on a structural ontology is to claim more than
   it has earned. The form-FORCED / value-INHERITED rule makes that
   pressure visible.

2. **It clarifies what falsifies ED.** A failed empirical number does
   not falsify ED, because ED never claimed the number. A failed
   structural form *does* falsify the relevant FORCED claim. This
   sharpens what counts as evidence.

3. **It keeps the framework portable.** Because forms are separated
   from values, the same primitive set can underwrite multiple
   empirical fillings without internal contradiction.

---

## 5. Why Violating the Rule Breaks the Ontology

A violation occurs when a document claims that a value is FORCED, or
that a form is INHERITED. Either reversal collapses the constitutional
boundary and damages the ontology.

If a *value* is claimed as FORCED:

- The primitives are then implicitly being credited with producing a
  number they do not produce.
- The Dimensional Atlas is bypassed, and with it the discipline that
  protects ED from circular self-confirmation.
- The framework loses its honest non-claim posture and becomes
  indistinguishable from an unfounded numerology.

If a *form* is claimed as INHERITED:

- A structural commitment that the primitives in fact entail is
  demoted to optional.
- The forcing argument that gives ED its claim to ontological status
  is silently abandoned.
- The framework loses its structural backbone and reduces to a list
  of empirical fits.

Either failure mode breaks the ontology. The rule is therefore
non-negotiable.

---

## 6. Application Discipline

In practice, the rule is enforced by labelling. Every substantive
claim in any ED document must wear one of the two tags. When the tag
is omitted, the default reading is FORCED for forms and INHERITED for
values; documents that risk ambiguity must label explicitly.

The labels are not decorative. They are the operational expression of
the constitutional boundary. A reader scanning any ED document should
be able to say, of every assertion: *is this a structural verdict, or
is this an empirical inheritance?* If the answer is unclear, the
document is in violation and must be revised.
