# why_event_based.md
### *Why ED uses events as the fundamental units of ontology*

ED is an **event‑based ontology**.  
This is a philosophical choice grounded in structural minimalism.

---

## 1. Objects assume too much
Objects presuppose:

- identity  
- persistence  
- boundaries  
- trajectories  

ED treats these as **derived**, not primitive.

---

## 2. Events are minimal
An event is:

- a structural node  
- a point of participation  
- a carrier of commitments  
- a locus of gradient interaction  

Events require no assumptions about:

- space  
- time  
- substance  
- continuity  

They are ontologically lean.

---

## 3. Events support relational structure
Events naturally form:

- chains  
- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  

These are the building blocks of ED architecture.

---

## 4. Events avoid metaphysical baggage
Events are not:

- experiences  
- processes  
- substances  
- particles  
- fields  

They are **structural positions** in the ED ontology.

---

## 5. Summary
ED uses events because:

- they are minimal  
- they are relational  
- they are compositional  
- they avoid hidden assumptions  
- they support the entire architecture  

Events are the **atoms of ED ontology**.
