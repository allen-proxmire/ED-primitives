# FORCED Theorems Inventory

**Status:** constitutional registry.
**Scope:** names only. No proofs, no physics content.

---

## 1. Purpose of This Inventory

This document is the constitutional-level registry of theorems
formally recognised as **FORCED** by the Event Density framework.

It records *that* a theorem exists and has been ratified. It does not
state the theorem, prove it, or discuss its physics content. The
substantive content of every entry below — its statement, its proof,
its consequences, its relationship to neighbouring results — lives
exclusively in the companion `event-density` repository.

The inventory exists so that the constitution can refer to the
FORCED-theorem layer by name, without importing physics-sector
material into this repository.

## 3. Where the Content Lives

Every theorem in this inventory has its full statement, derivation,
and discussion in the `event-density` repository, in the
corresponding arc memos and academic papers. This includes:

- the precise structural statement of the theorem;
- the chain of reasoning from the primitives;
- the refutation arguments that establish forcing (where applicable);
- the empirical compatibility analysis;
- the relationship to other FORCED theorems.

Nothing of that content is reproduced here. A reader who wants the
substance of a theorem must read the corresponding memos in
`event-density`.

---

## 4. Update Policy

This inventory is updated only when a new theorem is **formally
recognised** as FORCED. Recognition requires:

1. A complete derivation in the `event-density` repository.
2. Explicit identification of the structural argument that closes
   the forcing claim (typically a refutation of all admissible
   alternatives within the ontology).
3. A constitutional amendment, recorded in `CHANGELOG.md`, that
   adds the theorem to this list with the next available T-number.

A candidate result that is structurally interesting but not yet
formally FORCED does not appear here. It lives in the `event-density`
repository under whatever working status applies (CANDIDATE,
FORCED-conditional, ADMISSIBLE, etc.). Promotion to this inventory
is a constitutional event.

Demotion — removing a theorem from this list — is also a
constitutional event and requires a major-version amendment with
explicit rationale.
