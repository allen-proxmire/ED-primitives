# conceptual_background.md
### *The conceptual motivations behind ED*

This document explains the conceptual pressures that led to ED’s design.

---

## 1. Avoiding hidden assumptions
Most theories smuggle in:

- space  
- time  
- metric  
- continuity  
- substance  

ED avoids all of these.

---

## 2. Building from first principles
ED begins with:

- events  
- gradients  
- multiplicity  
- commitments  

These are **primitive**, not derived.

---

## 3. Ensuring structural clarity
ED requires:

- clean architecture  
- explicit commitments  
- minimal ingredients  
- compositional rules  

This ensures conceptual coherence.

---

## 4. Supporting emergent physics
ED is designed so that:

- physics can be layered on top  
- dynamics can be described later  
- observables can emerge from structure  

ED is the **substrate**, not the theory.

---

## 5. Summary
ED’s conceptual background is driven by:

- minimal assumptions  
- structural clarity  
- compositional richness  
- ontological discipline  

ED is built to be **clean, coherent, and foundational**.
