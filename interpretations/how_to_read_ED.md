# how_to_read_ED.md
### *A conceptual guide for newcomers*

This document explains how to approach ED materials.  
It is not a technical guide — it is a **mindset guide**.

---

## 1. Read ED as ontology, not physics
ED is about:

- what exists  
- how structure forms  
- how structure stabilizes  
- how structure propagates  

It is **not** about:

- particles  
- fields  
- forces  
- equations  
- experiments  

Those belong in the main ED research repo.

---

## 2. Focus on structure, not mechanism
ED is:

- structural  
- compositional  
- geometric  
- relational  

Do not look for:

- forces  
- mediators  
- interactions  
- causal mechanisms  

Instead, look for:

- gradients  
- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  
- commitments  

These are the **units of explanation**.

---

## 3. Follow the architectural pipeline
ED structure always follows the same sequence:

```
Sign‑Flip → Saddle → Boundary → Horizon → Motif → Network
```

This is the **universal architectural flow**.

If you understand this pipeline, you understand ED.

---

## 4. Treat primitives as conceptual atoms
The primitives are:

- minimal  
- irreducible  
- structural  
- non‑physical  

Do not interpret them as:

- particles  
- fields  
- operators  
- variables  

They are **conceptual ingredients**, not physical entities.

---

## 5. Ignore physics until the architecture is clear
Physics comes later.

If you try to map ED primitives onto physics too early, you will:

- misinterpret the ontology  
- collapse structure into mechanism  
- confuse motifs with particles  
- confuse boundaries with fields  

ED must be understood **on its own terms first**.

---

## 6. Look for patterns, not equations
ED is:

- pattern‑driven  
- motif‑driven  
- contrast‑driven  
- composition‑driven  

Equations are **descriptions**, not foundations.

---

## 7. Summary: How to read ED

- Read **structure**, not substance  
- Read **patterns**, not mechanisms  
- Read **relations**, not absolutes  
- Read **motifs**, not particles  
- Read **regimes**, not states  
- Read **coordination**, not time  
- Read **architecture**, not dynamics  

This is the mindset that makes ED coherent.
