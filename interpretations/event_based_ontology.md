# event_based_ontology.md
### *Why ED treats events as fundamental*

ED is an **event‑based ontology**.  
This means the basic units of existence are **events**, not objects, particles, or fields.

---

## 1. Objects are not primitive

Objects assume:

- identity  
- persistence  
- boundaries  
- trajectories  

ED treats all of these as **derived**, not fundamental.

Interpretation rule:

> **Objects are stabilized event patterns.**

This is why ED‑01 (micro‑event) is the first primitive.

---

## 2. Events are structural nodes

An event is:

- a point of participation  
- a carrier of commitments  
- a node in a chain  
- a locus of gradient interaction  

Events are not spacetime points.  
They are **structural positions** in the ED architecture.

---

## 3. Events precede objects, fields, and particles

In ED:

- particles = persistent motifs  
- fields = distributed motif regimes  
- objects = reinforced boundaries  
- waves = horizon‑driven propagation patterns  

All of these are **event‑composed**, not primitive.

---

## 4. Events precede time

Events do not occur *in* time.  
Instead:

> **Timing is the coordination of event chains.**

ED‑13 (relational timing) encodes this.

---

## 5. Events precede space

ED does not assume a background space.  
Spatial relations emerge from:

- boundaries  
- motifs  
- structural networks  

Space is **constructed**, not assumed.

---

## 6. Summary

To think event‑based:

- Think **nodes**, not objects.  
- Think **participation**, not persistence.  
- Think **chains**, not trajectories.  
- Think **coordination**, not time.  
- Think **structure**, not substance.  

Events are the **atoms of ontology** in ED.
