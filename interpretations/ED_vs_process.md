# ED_vs_process.md
### *How ED differs from process ontology*

ED is event‑based, but it is **not** a process ontology in the Whiteheadian or metaphysical sense.  
This document clarifies the distinction.

---

## 1. Process ontology treats processes as fundamental
Classical process metaphysics asserts:

- processes are primary  
- events are “drops of experience”  
- reality is fundamentally becoming  
- entities are stabilized processes  

ED does not adopt this framework.

---

## 2. ED treats events as structural, not experiential
In ED:

- events are **structural nodes**, not experiential units  
- events do not “flow” or “become”  
- events do not have internal temporal thickness  
- events do not carry metaphysical content  

Events are **positions in the architecture**, not metaphysical happenings.

---

## 3. ED does not treat process as primitive
Process ontologies begin with:

- becoming  
- unfolding  
- temporal flow  

ED does not.

ED begins with:

- gradients  
- contrast  
- commitments  
- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  

These are **structural**, not processual.

---

## 4. ED dynamics are architectural, not metaphysical
In ED:

- horizons encode direction  
- boundaries encode separation  
- motifs encode persistence  

These are **structural consequences**, not metaphysical processes.

ED dynamics emerge from **architecture**, not from metaphysical becoming.

---

## 5. ED is compositional, not processual
Process ontology emphasizes:

- continuity  
- flow  
- unfolding  

ED emphasizes:

- composition  
- motif formation  
- structural reinforcement  
- commitment propagation  

ED is about **how structure builds**, not how process flows.

---

## 6. Summary

ED differs from process ontology because:

- events are **structural**, not experiential  
- dynamics are **architectural**, not metaphysical  
- motifs are **compositional**, not processual  
- persistence is **boundary‑driven**, not process‑driven  
- timing is **coordination**, not flow  

ED is event‑based — but not process‑based.
