# ED vs Other Quantum Foundations

**Status:** ontology-level interpretive memo.
**Scope:** conceptual positioning only. No equations.

---

## 1. Purpose

This memo locates Event Density relative to the established families
of quantum-foundational programmes. The aim is conceptual
positioning, not technical comparison. No equations are reproduced;
no derivations are sketched. The question answered here is simply:
*how does the ED ontology stand in relation to the major
interpretive families, and why is ED not a member of any of them?*

The reader is assumed to have at least passing familiarity with the
named programmes. No exposition of those programmes is attempted.

---

## 2. Bohmian / Pilot-Wave

Bohmian mechanics adds a layer of definite particle trajectories
guided by a wave function that retains its standard role. The
ontology is dual: configurations and a guiding field.

ED differs at the ontological level. There is no guiding field
overlaid on a separate configuration; there are only events,
participation amplitudes, and the relational structures the
primitives compose from them. ED does not posit hidden particle
trajectories. Where Bohmian mechanics resolves the measurement
problem by positing definite positions throughout, ED resolves the
question of definiteness through *commitment* — a primitive that
operates on events rather than on positions of pre-existing
particles.

---

## 3. Everett / Many-Worlds

The Everett programme treats the wave function as the complete
ontology and reads apparent definiteness as a branching structure
within an unbranching universal state.

ED differs at the level of what the fundamental furniture of the
world is. The ED substrate is a discrete population of events
related by the primitives, not a continuous universal state vector.
ED does not require, and does not invoke, branching of an
encompassing state. The relational structures ED describes are
neither a single uncollapsed superposition nor a multiplicity of
parallel sheets; they are the structures that the primitives in fact
compose, and there is only one of them.

---

## 4. QBism / Quantum Bayesianism

QBism treats quantum states as personal degrees of belief held by
agents and reads quantum mechanics as a normative theory of how
agents should update those degrees of belief.

ED is not an epistemic theory. It is an ontology. The participation
amplitudes and relational structures of ED are not credences and are
not indexed to agents. Where QBism is agent-relative by design, ED
is agent-independent by design. The two programmes answer different
questions and disagree about which question is fundamental.

---

## 5. Relational Quantum Mechanics

RQM holds that the values of physical quantities are real only
relative to a system that has interacted with them; there is no
observer-independent fact of the matter about quantum properties.

ED shares with RQM a refusal to ground the world in absolute,
observer-independent property values. Beyond that surface
similarity, the two part company. RQM relativises *property values*
to interactions. ED relativises *nothing*; it instead specifies a
substrate (events, participation, gradients, commitment) within
which the appearance of property values arises. The ED substrate is
not relational in RQM's sense; it is *primitive* in a way RQM is
not.

---

## 6. Retrocausal Programmes

Retrocausal interpretations modify the temporal structure of
influence so that future boundary conditions can constrain present
events. The ontology is typically conventional; what changes is
the direction of causal arrows.

ED's primitive of relational timing does not implement retrocausality
in this technical sense. The temporal structure ED commits to is
relational rather than directed-from-the-future. ED neither requires
nor forbids retrocausal phenomena at the level of physics-sector
applications, but at the constitutional level the framework is not
a retrocausal interpretation. It is a structural ontology that does
not take ordinary forward causality as a primitive at all, and so
does not need to invert it.

---

## 7. Decoherent Histories

The decoherent-histories programme treats sets of consistent
histories as the bearers of probabilistic statements, with
decoherence selecting which sets are admissible.

ED is not a histories framework. The bearers of structure in ED are
events and the relations the primitives compose, not histories
selected by decoherence. ED does not centre on the question of which
sets of histories are consistent. That question is downstream of an
ontology that already presupposes a state and a Hilbert-space
structure; ED's primitives operate at a level beneath that
presupposition.

---

## 8. Why ED Is Not Any of These

ED is not a member of any of the families surveyed above. The reason
is structural, not stylistic.

Each of the surveyed programmes accepts, in some form, the standard
quantum-mechanical apparatus — wave functions, Hilbert spaces,
operators, states — and *interprets* it. They differ in what they
take that apparatus to refer to. ED, by contrast, does not begin
with the apparatus. It begins with primitives — events,
participation, gradients, commitment, relational timing, and so on
— and treats the standard apparatus as something to be located
downstream, in the physics sector, as one possible form-FORCED
construction over the substrate.

This is the constitutional difference. The interpretive families
answer the question *what does the quantum formalism describe?* ED
answers a different question: *what is the structural substrate
within which any such formalism can arise?* The two questions are
not in competition. They sit at different levels.

A consequence of this difference is that ED is, in principle,
compatible with several of the surveyed programmes as readings of
the *physics-sector layer* it underwrites — and incompatible with
others — without being identical to any of them. The constitution
of ED takes no position on which of the interpretive readings is
correct above the structural layer. It takes a strong position only
on what the structural layer itself is.
