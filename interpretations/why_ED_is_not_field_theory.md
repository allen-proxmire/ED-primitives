# why_ED_is_not_field_theory.md
### *Clarifying the distinction between ED and field-based ontologies*

ED is not a field theory.  
This document explains why.

---

## 1. Field theories assume a background
Field theories require:

- a manifold  
- coordinates  
- a metric  
- continuity  
- differentiability  

ED assumes none of these.

ED begins with:

- events  
- gradients  
- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  

These are **structural**, not field-like.

---

## 2. Field theories assume values assigned to points
A field assigns:

- numbers  
- vectors  
- tensors  

to points in space or spacetime.

ED has:

- no points  
- no coordinates  
- no background  
- no metric  

Events are **structural nodes**, not points in a manifold.

---

## 3. Field theories assume smoothness
Fields require:

- continuity  
- differentiability  
- smooth variation  

ED does not.

ED structure is:

- compositional  
- motif-driven  
- contrast-based  
- regime-defined  

Smoothness is not a primitive.

---

## 4. Field theories assume substance
Fields are often interpreted as:

- physical media  
- energetic distributions  
- continuous substances  

ED assumes **no substance**.

Structure is primary.

---

## 5. Summary

ED is not a field theory because:

- it has no background manifold  
- it has no coordinate system  
- it has no metric  
- it has no field values  
- it has no smoothness assumptions  
- it treats structure, not substance, as fundamental  

ED is **structural**, not field-based.
