# why_ED_is_not_wavefunction_theory.md
### *Why ED is not a wavefunction ontology*

ED is not a wavefunction theory.  
It does not interpret the world as a state in a Hilbert space.

---

## 1. Wavefunction theories assume a state space
Wavefunction ontologies require:

- a Hilbert space  
- a state vector  
- amplitudes  
- phases  
- operators  

ED assumes none of these.

---

## 2. Wavefunction theories assume evolution laws
Wavefunctions evolve via:

- Schrödinger equation  
- Lindblad equation  
- unitary evolution  

ED has **no primitive evolution law**.  
Dynamics emerge from **horizons**, not from equations.

---

## 3. Wavefunction theories assume superposition
Superposition is a mathematical structure.

ED does not assume:

- linearity  
- superposition  
- amplitudes  
- interference  

These belong to physics, not ontology.

---

## 4. Wavefunction theories assume measurement structure
Wavefunction ontologies require:

- observables  
- eigenstates  
- collapse rules  
- measurement postulates  

ED has none of these primitives.

---

## 5. Summary

ED is not a wavefunction theory because:

- it has no Hilbert space  
- it has no state vector  
- it has no operators  
- it has no superposition  
- it has no measurement postulates  
- it is not a dynamical theory  

ED is **structural ontology**, not quantum ontology.
