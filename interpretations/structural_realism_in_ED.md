# structural_realism_in_ED.md
### *How ED relates to structural realism (without physics)*

ED is compatible with structural realism — but only in a **structural, not physical** sense.

---

## 1. Structural realism claims structure is primary
Structural realism asserts:

- relations matter more than objects  
- structure is more fundamental than substance  
- identity is derivative  

ED agrees with this — but with important differences.

---

## 2. ED is structural without being physical
Structural realism is often tied to:

- physical symmetries  
- physical laws  
- physical invariants  

ED is **pre‑physical**.

Its structures are:

- motifs  
- boundaries  
- horizons  
- commitments  

These are **ontological**, not physical.

---

## 3. ED is structural without being mathematical
Structural realism often treats structure as:

- group theory  
- category theory  
- algebraic structure  

ED does not.

ED structure is:

- geometric  
- relational  
- compositional  
- motif‑driven  

It is not mathematical in origin.

---

## 4. ED is structural without being epistemic
Structural realism sometimes claims:

- we only know structure  
- objects are unknowable  
- structure is epistemic  

ED is not epistemic.  
It is **ontological**.

ED claims:

> **Structure is what exists, not what we know.**

---

## 5. Summary

ED aligns with structural realism because:

- structure is primary  
- relations define existence  
- objects are derivative  

But ED differs because:

- its structure is **ontological**, not physical  
- its structure is **geometric**, not algebraic  
- its structure is **pre‑theoretic**, not epistemic  

ED is a **structural ontology**, not a structural physics theory.
